About Us

General Counsel Advisory was founded by Julie Glasser, a seasoned legal executive with more than 15 years of law firm and in-house experience at publicly traded companies. The firm provides critical legal guidance to growing and mid-size companies that need practical, business-focused legal leadership without the cost or commitment of hiring in-house counsel. Julie delivers direct answers, commercial judgment and actionable advice tied to business priorities. Clients engage her as a fractional general counsel, as senior backup for a lean legal team or for a single project during periods of growth or change.

Julie's practice spans corporate governance, commercial contracts, compliance, employment, litigation and dispute management, and legal operations. She has served as the senior lawyer responsible for supporting business development, sales, human resources, supply chain, finance, information technology, research and development, clinical affairs, procurement, investor relations and corporate communications. Julie is a Certified Compliance and Ethics Professional and a Certified Corporate Governance Professional. She earned her J.D. from Columbia Law School and holds a B.A. and M.A. from Stanford University.

Why Hire a Fractional General Counsel?

  • You need senior legal judgment but can't yet justify a full-time general counsel.
  • Your legal needs cross practice areas and change from month to month.
  • You want predictable legal spend instead of surprise invoices from outside counsel.
  • You're heading into a financing, an acquisition or another high-stakes transaction.
  • You want legal counsel who advised leadership and operational teams at public companies and gives answers in business terms.
  • You'd rather not recruit, manage and pay for an in-house legal department yet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about fractional general counsel services.

What is a fractional general counsel?

A fractional general counsel is an experienced lawyer who serves as your company's general counsel on a part-time basis. You get the same kind of support a full-time GC provides, including contracts, board and governance matters, compliance, employment issues, coordination of outside counsel and proactive risk management, for a fraction of the cost.

How is a fractional GC different from hiring a law firm?

A law firm is typically hired matter by matter and bills by the hour. A fractional general counsel works as a seamless extension of your team, learns your business, spots issues before they become problems, manages risk proactively and brings in specialist firms only when a matter truly calls for one.

What does a fractional general counsel cost?

Far less than a full-time hire. General Counsel Advisory offers time-based, flat-rate fees and project-based pricing sized to the hours you actually need, without billing surprises or long-term overhead.

What kinds of companies does General Counsel Advisory work with?

Startups, high-growth and mid-market companies across industries. Julie has deep experience in life sciences, diagnostics and technology, and the core work of a general counsel translates across sectors: transactions, governance, compliance, employment and legal operations.
